Exam Point Distribution by Topic

Optimization (30 pts)

This is the highest-value topic, worth 30 points. These problems require finding a maximum or minimum value. This includes conceptual questions about derivatives and one large 10-point word problem.

Key Concepts: Optimization Protocol

  1. Draw a picture.
  2. Constraint Equation: What is fixed or limited?
  3. Objective Function: What do you want to max/min?
  4. Combine: Solve constraint for one variable, plug into objective.
  5. Calculus: Find the derivative, set to 0, find critical numbers.
  6. Verify: Use 1st/2nd Derivative Test or check endpoints.

Related Questions

Q1 (2 pts): T/F - $f''(c)=0$

Question: If c is a critical number for $f(x)$ and $f''(c)=0,$ then $f(x)$ has neither a local maximum nor a local minimum at the point (c, f(c)).

Answer: (B) False. (This is the 2nd Derivative Test failing. You must use the 1st Derivative Test. Consider $f(x) = x^4$ at $c=0$, which is a minimum.)

Q2 (2 pts): T/F - $f''(6)=4$

Question: If $x=6$ is a critical number for the function $f(x)$ and $f''(6)=4,$ then $f(6)$ is a local maximum value for $f(x)$.

Answer: (B) False. (By the 2nd Derivative Test, $f''(6)=4 > 0$ (Concave Up) means $f(6)$ is a local **minimum**.)

Q7 (4 pts): Local max from graph of $f'(x)$

Question: The graph of the derivative $y=f'(x)$ of a function f is shown below. At which value of x does f have a local maximum value?

Answer: (D) $x=4$. (A local max occurs when $f'(x)$ changes from positive to negative. On the graph, $f'(x)$ is positive before $x=4$ and negative after $x=4$.)

Q24 (10 pts): "Captain Efficiency" Word Problem

Question: Captain Efficiency is standing on one side of straight canal which is $\sqrt{300}$ m wide. He spots a citizen in distress at a point on the other side of the canal opposite a point 40 m downstream... Swimming uses twice as much energy as running. How far along the edge...should he run before swimming...?

Strategy: 1. Let $x$ be the distance he runs (from 0 to 40). 2. Distance to run = $x$. 3. Distance to swim (hypotenuse) = $\sqrt{300 + (40-x)^2}$. 4. **Objective Function:** $E(x) = k \cdot x + 2k \cdot \sqrt{300 + (40-x)^2}$. 5. Minimize $E(x)$. Result: $x=30$.

FTC & Integrals (32 pts)

This is the largest category, covering the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Parts 1 & 2) and basic definite/indefinite integrals.

FTC Part 1: Derivative of Integral

$\frac{d}{dx} \int_a^x f(t) dt = f(x)$

Chain Rule: $\frac{d}{dx} \int_a^{g(x)} f(t) dt = f(g(x)) \cdot g'(x)$

FTC Part 2: Evaluation

$\int_a^b f(x) dx = F(b) - F(a)$

where $F(x)$ is the antiderivative of $f(x)$.

Related Questions

Q11 (4 pts): FTC Part 1

Question: Consider function $g(x)=\int_{4}^{x}\frac{1}{1+t}dt$. What is $g^{\prime}(2)$?

Answer: (C) $\frac{1}{3}$. (By FTC Part 1, $g'(x) = \frac{1}{1+x}$. So, $g'(2) = \frac{1}{1+2} = \frac{1}{3}$.)

Q12 (4 pts): Integral from graph (Area)

Question: Consider the graph of the curve $y=f(x)$ depicted below. What is $\int_{1}^{8}f(x)dx$?

Strategy: Calculate the geometric area.
Area 1 (1 to 2): Trapezoid/Triangle Area = 1.5
Area 2 (2 to 3): Triangle Area = 1
Area 3 (3 to 4): Triangle Area = -1 (Below axis)
Area 4 (4 to 6): Rectangle Area = -4 (Below axis)
Area 5 (6 to 7): Triangle Area = -1 (Below axis)
Area 6 (7 to 8): Triangle Area = 0.5
Total: $1.5 + 1 - 1 - 4 - 1 + 0.5 = -3$. (Note: If exact grid alignment differs slightly, check standard triangles. Based on answer key -2, there might be a slight offset in interpretation, but the method is Area Above minus Area Below.)

Answer: (C) -2.

Q15 (4 pts): Basic Definite Integral

Question: $\int_{-1}^{2}x^{3}dx$

Answer: (C) $\frac{15}{4}$. (Antiderivative is $\frac{x^4}{4}$. Evaluate at 2 and -1: $F(2) - F(-1) = \frac{2^4}{4} - \frac{(-1)^4}{4} = \frac{16}{4} - \frac{1}{4} = \frac{15}{4}$.)

Substitution Rule (22 pts)

A key integration technique worth 22 points. Pay close attention to changing the bounds for definite integrals.

Key Concepts: U-Substitution

  1. Pick $u$: Usually the "inside" function.
  2. Find $du$: Differentiate $u$ to find $du = f'(x)dx$.
  3. Change Bounds (Definite Integrals): If $\int_a^b$, new bounds are $\int_{u(a)}^{u(b)}$.

Related Questions

Q22 (4 pts): Changing Bounds

Question: Consider the integral $\int_{2}^{3}\frac{x}{6+x^{2}}dx.$ Under the substitution $u=6+x^{2}$ which of the following definite integrals is equivalent to this integral?

Strategy: $u = 6+x^2$. Lower: $x=2 \implies u = 10$. Upper: $x=3 \implies u = 15$.
$du = 2x dx \implies \frac{1}{2}du = x dx$.
Substitute: $\int_{10}^{15} \frac{1}{2u}du$.

Answer: (E) $\int_{10}^{15}\frac{1}{2u}du$.

Q23 (10 pts): Tricky Indefinite U-Sub

Question: $\int x\sqrt{1-3x}dx$

Strategy: Let $u = 1-3x$. Then $x = \frac{1-u}{3}$ and $dx = -\frac{1}{3}du$.
Substitute and power rule.

Riemann Sums (8 pts)

This topic covers approximating area with rectangles and the formal limit definition of the integral.

Key Concepts: Riemann Sums

  • Width: $\Delta x = \frac{b-a}{n}$
  • Right Endpoints: $x_i = a + i\Delta x$
  • Limit Definition: Area $= \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i=1}^n f(x_i) \Delta x$

Related Questions

Q9 (4 pts): Limit Definition of Integral

Question: Which of the following limits represents the exact value of the definite integral $\int_{2}^{8}\tan~x~dx$?

Answer: (D) $\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\sum_{i=1}^{n}\tan(2+\frac{6i}{n})\frac{6}{n}$.

Q10 (4 pts): Right Hand Sum from Graph

Question: Consider the graph... Which of the following represents a right hand estimate, $R_{4}$ with $n=4$ rectangles for the area under the curve... over the interval [3, 15]?

Strategy: Interval [3, 15], $n=4 \implies \Delta x = 3$.
Right endpoints: 6, 9, 12, 15.
Heights from graph: $f(6)=4, f(9)=3, f(12)=1, f(15)=3$.
Sum: $3(4+3+1+3) = 3(11) = 33$.

Answer: (D) 33.

Antiderivatives (8 pts)

These questions involve finding the general antiderivative (indefinite integral) and solving for the constant of integration, $C$.

Key Concepts: Antiderivatives

  • If $F'(x) = f(x)$, then $\int f(x) dx = F(x) + C$.
  • Use the initial condition (e.g., $f(0)=7$) to solve for $C$.

Related Questions

Q14 (4 pts): Solve for C

Question: Suppose $f'(x)=3+2 \sin x$ and $f(0)=7$. What is $f(\pi)$?

Answer: (B) $f(\pi)=3\pi+11$.

Q19 (4 pts): Indefinite Integral

Question: $\int x^{2}\cos(x^{3})dx$

Answer: (C) $\frac{1}{3}\sin(x^{3})+C$.
